## Project Management Process Summary

OctoAcme’s approach is built around five core principles: customer-first value, iterative development, clear ownership, data-informed decisions, and psychological safety. Every project follows a structured lifecycle: Initiation, Planning, Execution, Release, and Retrospective/Improvement.

Projects start with a one-pager outlining scope, success metrics, and stakeholders. During planning, a prioritized backlog is developed, dependencies and risks identified, and sprint/release planning completed. Execution focuses on daily standups, weekly syncs, and tracking progress in project boards with clear states (Backlog, In Progress, QA, Done). Pull requests drive incremental delivery—kept small, reviewed, and validated through automation and manual QA. Quality is assured through unit, integration, and end-to-end tests, with CI pipelines for security and correctness.

Risk is proactively managed using a register that’s discussed weekly, with escalation paths clearly defined from team to sponsor. Communication cadence includes weekly PM–PdM syncs, regular team standups, monthly stakeholder updates, and frequent documentation updates. After every major effort, retrospectives capture learnings and actionable improvements.

Roles are sharply defined:  
- **Project Manager:** Coordinates delivery, schedules, risk, and communications.  
- **Product Manager:** Owns priorities, outcomes, and measurement.  
- **Developers:** Implement, test, and document features.  
- **QA/Testing:** Ensure acceptance criteria, quality, and readiness for release.

This collaborative, documentation-driven process ensures rapid onboarding, consistent execution, and continuous improvement.
